Welcome to CHP Management

Update #1 · Launch Guide
Everything the bot does, and what it means for your rank.
CHP Management is the all-in-one Discord bot for California Highway Patrol's roleplay community - shifts, recruitment, RA training, leave of absence, promotions, and an AI assistant that knows your department's policy, all in one place. This guide walks through what it does, what changed from the base ERM project it grew out of, and exactly what each rank can do with it.

Best Features

The highlights - if you only remember a handful of things about this bot, make it these.

🤖

/ask - AI Assistant

Ask anything about CHP - ranks, codes, policy, vehicles - and get an answer pulled straight from the official knowledge base, not a guess.

📝

In-House Applications

A full DM-based application flow with AI-assisted review, AI detection, background checks, and history - all from one embed, no spreadsheets.

⚙️

/settings - Truly Personal

Timezone, accent color, notification timing, profile widgets - nobody but you can change your own settings, not even Command.

🎖️

Automatic Promotions & Discipline

Meeting quota can automatically move you up the rank ladder; missing it automatically escalates a Warning/Strike track - no manual role-juggling for Command.

🏅

Earned Badges on /profile

Medals, ribbons, service stripes, and Trooper of the Week all show up automatically on your profile the moment you earn them - no manual setup per badge.

🔍

Universal Search

One search command to find a member's rank, duty status, applications, and RA history without hopping between five different commands.

All Features

Shifts & Duty

  • Clock on/off duty, take breaks, and view your stats with /duty manage.
  • See everyone currently on duty with /duty active.
  • Your own duty time, shift history, and all-time profile via /duty time, /duty history, /duty profile.
  • Weekly leaderboard, quota tracking, and a progress bar right on /profile.
  • Per-rank quotas, configurable shift types, and an optional confirmation before ending unusually long shifts.
  • A weekly duty leaderboard automatically logged to a channel (and optionally a Google Sheet) every time the week resets.

Leave of Absence

  • File, view, and track your own LOA with /loa request and /loa view.
  • Admin tier can review and administrate anyone's LOA.

RA Training

  • FTOs host individual or bulk training sessions and submit pass/fail results.
  • Probationary Officers request training, which pings every FTO.
  • RA leaderboard, history, and anonymous FTO feedback ratings.
  • Passing an RA session automatically grants the Officer role and updates your nickname.
  • Auto-toggle your RA availability based on duty status, with a one-click Available/Unavailable button for FTOs.

Applications & Recruitment

  • A DM-based application flow with progress auto-saved if you time out mid-application.
  • Hicom can Claim, Accept, or Decline, run an AI Detector and Summary, check applicant history and background, and leave notes - all from the application embed.
  • Automatic setup resolves every recruitment role and channel, creating anything that's missing.
  • Weekly recruitment activity reports.

Promotions & Discipline

  • Weekly promotion list (Probationary Officer through Senior Sergeant) and quota enforcement list, both reviewable before anything sends.
  • Choose per-list whether roles are applied automatically, the message is sent automatically, both, or neither - full control over how automated discipline and promotions actually are.
  • Automatic Warning 1 → Warning 2 → Strike 1 → Strike 2 escalation for missed quota, with a non-pinging alert posted for Command once someone reaches the top of the ladder.
  • Monthly High Rank promotion review based on the last 5 weeks of duty activity.
  • Automatic service stripe progression based on tenure - no manual role updates.

Profile, Settings & Personalization

  • /profile - hours vs quota, shift streak, RA stats, and every earned badge.
  • /settings - private profile, timezone (accepts IANA names, abbreviations, UTC offsets, or city names), accent color, mobile-friendly embeds, notification timing, and more - paginated and permission-gated by rank.
  • /myactions - your own recent application reviews and RA results.

Reference Tools

  • /ask - AI assistant for CHP policy questions.
  • /info - codes, penal/vehicle codes, loadout, and FAQ.
  • /practice - a quick quiz to test yourself on codes and policy.
  • /lookup - check anyone's rank, badge number, on-duty status, and join date.
  • A universal search command across members, applications, and RA history.

Configuration

  • /config - organized into focused, paginated pages (Roles, Channels & Quotas, RA Training, Recruitment, Applications) with plain-language explanations instead of one long dropdown.
  • A distinct Admin Role (shift/LOA management without full Commissioner access) and Internal Affairs Role (can view private profiles for investigations) alongside Staff, Management, and HR roles.

Improvements Compared to ERM

CHP Management is built on the open-source ERM project, but a large amount has changed since. The short version: faster, more reliable, and a lot of features ERM never had.

New, not in base ERM

  • The entire AI Assistant (/ask), AI application detector/summary, and background check tools.
  • The full in-house DM application system, replacing external form links.
  • Personal /settings - private profile, timezone, accent color, notification timing.
  • Automatic promotion and infraction role ladders.
  • Automatic service stripe progression and generic badge detection (medals, ribbons, Trooper of the Week).
  • Admin Role and Internal Affairs Role as distinct permission tiers.
  • Weekly duty leaderboard logging to a channel and Google Sheets.
  • /myactions, universal search, /dashboard, /lookup, /practice, /info.

Fixed or hardened since launch

  • Startup speed: dependency cleanup, parallel extension loading, and a command-sync cache so restarts skip unnecessary network round trips.
  • Shift leaderboard caching instead of recomputing on every single read.
  • A permission bug where Admin tier (Command/HR) couldn't view another staff member's duty profile unless they were also a Commissioner - despite that being core to an HR's job.
  • A pagination bug in /config that could silently fail to save role/channel changes.
  • Dual-shape settings data (some fields stored as a single role vs. a list depending on which setup flow was used) normalized everywhere it's read, instead of crashing.
  • Stale/crashed shifts left open 16+ hours now auto-close instead of getting stuck forever.

For Probationary Officers

What you can do

  • Request RA training with /ra request - this pings every FTO so you don't have to track anyone down individually.
  • Check your own duty time, shift history, and profile with /duty time and /profile.
  • Customize your own experience in /settings - timezone, accent color, mobile-friendly embeds, and whether you get inactivity check-in DMs.
  • Passing your RA session automatically grants you the Officer role and updates your nickname - no need to ask anyone.

For Deputies

What you can do

  • Manage your own shifts end-to-end with /duty manage - clock on/off, take breaks, see your stats.
  • File and track your own Leave of Absence with /loa request and /loa view.
  • See who's currently on duty with /duty active, and the team-wide leaderboard with /duty leaderboard.
  • A full extra tier of personal settings in /settings - default shift type, your own quota reminder timing, a confirmation prompt before ending unusually long shifts, default RA trainee cap, and which widgets show on your profile.
  • Earn badges automatically - medals, ribbons, service stripes, and Trooper of the Week all show up on /profile the moment a Discord role is granted, no extra step needed.
  • Get automatically promoted (Probationary Officer through Senior Sergeant) just by meeting quota - no need to ask Command if a weekly auto-promotion run is enabled.

For Field Training Officers

What you can do

  • Host individual or bulk RA sessions with /ra host, and submit pass/fail results with /ra results.
  • Toggle your training availability with a one-click button - it can also auto-toggle alongside your duty status if you turn that on in /settings.
  • Check the RA leaderboard, search session history, and see your own anonymous feedback ratings.
  • Opt into a DM whenever a trainee you worked with gets a verdict on their application, so you find out without having to ask.
  • Command and High Rank Team can also step into any FTO tool without holding the FTO tag themselves - so oversight never requires re-tagging anyone.

For High Ranks & Command

What you can do

  • View any staff member's duty profile, time, and history - including someone with a Private Profile set, since reviewing duty activity is exactly what this tier exists for.
  • Administrate shifts and LOAs for anyone with /duty admin and /loa admin - force-end, void, or edit a shift, or step in on someone's leave request.
  • Run the monthly High Rank promotion review with /duty hrreview, based on the last 5 weeks of logged activity.
  • Decide what happens once a Deputy reaches the top of the infraction ladder (Strike 2) - the bot flags it for you instead of guessing what discipline should follow.
  • Everything a Deputy can do, plus all of the above - this tier stacks, it doesn't replace.

For the Office of Commissioners

What you can do

  • Full access to /config and /setup - every role, channel, quota, and recruitment setting, organized into focused pages instead of one long list.
  • Control exactly how automated promotions and discipline are: choose per-list whether roles are applied automatically, the announcement is sent automatically, both, or neither.
  • Reset the week, review the promotion and quota enforcement lists, and trigger the official announcements with /duty quota.
  • Configure the Admin Role and Internal Affairs Role as their own permission tiers, separate from full Commissioner access.
  • Set up the weekly duty leaderboard log (channel + optional Google Sheet) so a permanent record exists every week without anyone having to remember to export it.
  • Hicom-tier access to the full application queue, AI-assisted review tools, and recruitment reports.
