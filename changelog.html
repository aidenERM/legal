New Feature

CHP Management Launch - Recruitment, Applications, /ask & a Big QOL Pass

Update #1 · 2026-06-26

The first full release: automatic recruitment setup, a complete in-house application system with AI-assisted review, an AI assistant that knows CHP policy, a personal /settings command, an extra layer of tools for Deputies, and a long list of quality-of-life improvements across shifts and applications.

  • Automatic Setup resolves the Hicom, Officer, Probationary Officer, Denied, and Blacklisted roles, plus the applications-log, apply, hicom-announcements, and recruitment-report channels - creating anything that's missing.
  • Passing an RA session now also grants the Officer role and swaps PO to O in the trainee's nickname.
  • /config is reorganized into focused pages (Roles, Channels & Quotas, RA Training, Recruitment) with plain-language explanations instead of one long dropdown.
  • New application system: a DM-based pre-screening flow (privacy policy, background check, required reading, AI warning) followed by 23 application questions, with progress automatically saved if you time out mid-application.
  • Staff can Claim, Accept, or Decline applications, run an AI Detector and Summary, check applicant history and background, flag for priority review, leave notes, and view a reapplication diff - all from the application embed.
  • /ask - ask the bot anything about CHP (ranks, codes, policy, vehicles) and it answers using the official CHP knowledge base.
  • /profile - shows your weekly hours vs quota, shift streak, RA pass rate, and earned badges.
  • /dashboard - a management overview combining shift, RA, and recruitment activity in one place.
  • Accepted applicants now get a step-by-step welcome guide instead of a plain acceptance message.
  • Automatic weekly report card DMs, quota reminders for anyone falling behind, and a weekly recruitment report.
  • /settings - a personal settings command nobody else can change for you: private profile (hide your duty time from non-Hicom staff and leaderboards), timezone, mobile-friendly embeds, and opt-outs for inactivity nudges and the weekly report card.
  • Deputy-only settings (visible to Staff Role holders): a default shift type so you don't have to pick one every time, your own quota reminder day/time instead of the server default, an optional confirmation before ending unusually long shifts, auto-claiming applications you open, a default max-trainee cap for bulk RA sessions, auto-toggling your RA availability when you go on/off duty, which /profile widgets you see, and DM alerts for new applications or when a trainee of yours gets a verdict.
  • /myactions - a Deputy-only scoped view of your own recent application reviews and RA results, without needing full audit access.
  • /info - a dropdown reference for 10 codes, penal/vehicle codes, loadout, vehicle regulations, and FAQ.
  • /practice - a 5-question quiz pulled from codes and policy to test yourself anytime.
  • /lookup - quickly check anyone's rank, badge number, on-duty status, and join date.
  • A gentler daily check-in DM for anyone inactive a few days, separate from the harsher quota reminder, and smart enough to skip people who've been busy training recruits instead of logging a shift.
  • /profile now shows a quota progress bar and how much more time you need for your next promotion, and respects a grace period so a mid-week promotion doesn't retroactively flag you for missing a quota you only just inherited.
  • Shift fixes: a clear "you're already on duty" message instead of silently doing nothing, shifts left open 16+ hours from a crash now auto-close instead of getting stuck forever, an optional note you can leave when ending a shift, and paginated shift history in /duty time.
  • Application fixes: a review-and-confirm screen before final submission, a heads-up when you're running low on time to answer a question, and an Apply Again button if you accidentally decline the Privacy Policy step (no cooldown applies).
  • /ask is now capped at one request every 30 seconds bot-wide to keep AI costs in check, and the application flow skips its own Privacy Policy step if you've already agreed to the bot's Terms/Privacy Policy elsewhere.
  • Refreshed embed styling using the server's own custom emojis across Applications, /profile, and /dashboard.
